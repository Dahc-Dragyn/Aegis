1. The "Compliance-as-Code" Sentinel (Enterprise B2B)
Companies working with the government (or those needing NIST/DoD RMF compliance) spend thousands of man-hours auditing logs to ensure they meet security controls.

The Idea: A specialized version of your Log Sentinel that doesn't just "notify" you of errors, but maps log events directly to NIST SP 800-53 controls. It generates real-time compliance "Heartbeats."

The "Fast Money": You sell this as a lightweight, binary-executable "Audit-in-a-Box." Small defense contractors would pay a premium for a tool that automates their quarterly reporting.

The Rust Edge: Rust’s memory safety is a huge selling point for Infosec. "The tool itself can't be exploited via buffer overflows" is a major "Primo" pitch.

Vortex Test: Gate 4 (Audit) becomes the most important part of this build.
